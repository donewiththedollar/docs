# VOIDX Vaults

Vaults are the fastest way to start a bot on VOIDX. Each vault card is a curated, pre-configured strategy: pick the card that matches your exchange and risk appetite, optionally tweak the symbol, and click **Deploy Vault**. The strategy launches through the standard deploy wizard, prefilled with the vault's settings, running on your own exchange API keys.

Your funds never leave your exchange account. Vaults are **non-custodial** — the platform only places and manages orders on your behalf through read/trade-only API keys. You retain custody of funds at all times.

## What You See

The Vaults tab header shows two counters: **Available** (the number of vaults in the catalog — currently 14) and **Your Live** (how many of them you have running right now).

Each vault card carries:

- **Name** and a one-paragraph description of the strategy
- **Exchange badge** with a colored dot (BloFin blue, Bybit gold)
- **Strategy badge** — Professional MM, GLFT Optimal, or Vortex DCA Waves
- **Risk tier badge** — Conservative (green) or Moderate (amber)
- **Min balance** — anchored top-right; the suggested minimum futures-wallet balance
- **Symbol picker** — single-symbol vaults let you pick from popular symbols (BTC, ETH, SOL, XRP, ASTER, SUI, DOGE, LINK, AVAX) or type a custom one before deploying; multi-symbol vaults list their pairs (editable in the deploy modal)
- **Stats row** — Equity (per-trade or wallet exposure), Levels, Risk
- **Features line** — the strategy features bundled into the preset
- **Deploy Vault** button — or **Connect wallet to deploy** for guests

Vortex vaults add two extra controls directly on the card:

- **Wallet Exposure slider** — override the preset's default exposure (70% single-symbol, 60% anchored, 30% per symbol on multi)
- **Leverage slider** — only applied if you actually move it; left untouched, the bot keeps your exchange's current leverage setting

If a vault already has a running bot, the card shows a green **Live** badge and the button becomes **Active — Deploy Another**.

## Getting Started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your wallet">
    Open the **Vaults** tab and connect a wallet — this is your identity on the platform. Signing the authentication message moves no funds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add exchange API keys (once)">
    If you haven't already, save your exchange API keys via the Add Exchange flow. Keys are stored encrypted.

    <Warning>Only enable **Read** and **Trade** permissions on your API key. Never enable **Withdraw**.</Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a vault">
    Choose a card matching your exchange and risk appetite. Adjust the symbol (and, for Vortex vaults, exposure/leverage) if you want.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deploy">
    Click **Deploy Vault**. The deploy wizard opens prefilled with the vault's configuration — confirm and the bot starts trading.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Vault Features Explained

**Drawdown spacing** — when a position is underwater, grid spacing widens automatically so the bot doesn't pile into a losing position too aggressively.

**Inventory balancing** — quotes skew toward reducing inventory when the position grows too one-sided, keeping the book balanced over time.

**Geometric levels** — grid levels are spaced exponentially rather than evenly, committing less capital far from price.

**Reactive quoting** — spread width adapts to live volatility and order flow: tighter in calm markets, wider in fast ones.

**Profit chase (2bp)** — instead of closing exactly at the take-profit target, the bot trails price by 2 basis points to capture extra upside.

**Profit floor** — once a position reaches a minimum profit, the bot will not let it fall back below that level.

**GLFT optimal quoting** — bid/ask offsets computed from the GLFT closed-form solution using volatility, order arrival rate, and risk aversion — no heuristic tuning.

**Inventory penalty** — a gamma-based penalty that skews GLFT quotes to shed position risk as inventory grows.

**Regime detection** — classifies the market as ranging, trending, or volatile (via ADX) and adjusts spreads accordingly.

**Recovery grid** — orderbook-aware DCA that places recovery orders at support/resistance walls with escalating size.

**Auto fee detection** — Bybit vaults read your actual fee tier instead of assuming hardcoded rates.

**Auto max waves / wave queue** — Vortex vaults resolve their wave count at startup from your live wallet balance × exposure, and extend the grid with rescue waves if price keeps falling.

**Anchor-stable prices** — anchored Vortex vaults pin wave prices to an activation anchor and tolerate small drift per order instead of constantly cancelling and replacing — far less order churn.

## Managing a Deployed Vault

A vault deployment is a standard VOIDX bot. After deploying:

- The vault card shows **Live**; the button becomes **Active — Deploy Another**
- The bot appears in the **[Bots](/features/bot-management)** tab with live status, PnL, uptime, and Start/Stop/Settings/Delete controls
- Its PnL is tracked in **[Portfolio](/features/portfolio)** alongside your other bots
- Click the bot's **Settings** gear to customize any parameter — at that point it is your configuration to manage

<Warning>Stopping a vault bot does not close its open positions. Close those from the Trade tab or your exchange if needed.</Warning>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Are my funds safe?">
  Your funds never leave your exchange account. Vaults place and manage orders through read/trade-only API keys; the platform cannot withdraw. Your wallet connection is identity only — no tokens or funds pass through it.

  For maximum security: never enable withdrawal permissions, use IP whitelisting if your exchange offers it, and enable 2FA on your exchange account.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is the difference between Vaults and the Marketplace?">
  Both deploy the same kind of bot through the same wizard. **Vaults** are the curated shortlist — 14 picks with sensible defaults and minimal knobs. The **Marketplace** is the full catalog of 41 presets with full parameter detail and exchange variant toggles. Start with Vaults; graduate to the Marketplace when you want more choice.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I change the symbol before deploying?">
  Yes. Single-symbol vaults have a symbol picker on the card itself — pick a popular symbol or type a custom one. Multi-symbol vaults list their default pairs, editable in the deploy modal.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I customize a vault after deployment?">
  Yes. The deployed bot is a standard VOIDX bot — open its Settings from the Bots tab and adjust anything. Once changed, the configuration is yours to manage.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What do the wallet exposure and leverage sliders do?">
  They only appear on Vortex vaults. **Wallet exposure** sets what share of your balance the wave ladder may use. **Leverage** is applied only if you actually move the slider — left untouched, the bot keeps whatever leverage your exchange account already has, so existing settings are never silently overridden.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What are the minimum balances?">
  Each card shows its own minimum, from **$50** (single-symbol Vortex vaults) up to **$2,000** (Bybit Multi-Symbol MM). These minimums ensure orders are large enough to execute; running with less may produce orders below exchange minimums.
</Accordion>
